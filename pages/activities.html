"Great 4 My Life" is the new project of SOLOMON association, project which will roll out between 1 to 11 September in Brasov .

During this project, there will be participating teenagers between 18 to 26 years old from Bulgary, Italy, Hungary, Lithuania, Turkey and Romania

×

Great 4 My Life

The main need and the general goal from which we developed the project “Great 4 MY Life”, according also with the general targets of the Erasmus+ program, is developing a lifestyle that is healthy, active, tolerant and involved.

The specific objectives are:

1. Increasing the level of awareness to the importance of a healthy diet to our 42 participants (35 youngsters and 7 group leaders) from Bulgaria, Italy, Lithuania, Malta, Hungary, Turkey and Romania during the 11 days of activities

2. Understanding the importance of interaction and communication in community

3. Understanding the benefits of using sport such as an instrument of social inclusion

Youngsters will develop basic competences of a lifestyle that is healthy, active, tolerant and involved and will have the possibilities to interact through the opportunities offered by the Erasmus+ program. The project will be based on informal and non-formal learning methods, will be participatory, experimental and will allow working in groups in interactive workshops.

Youngsters will have the opportunity to discuss and to understand the importance and necessity of their involvement on long term in the problems of a healthy lifestyle, to find and even implement practical solutions for improving quality of life.

Youth will be introduced to examples of good practice and new diet habits, so they will understand that marginalization and stereotypes can be exceeded, they will make physical activities and will learn to work in mix international teams.

The project wants to put an accent on finding ideas that are creative and innovative through realizing a video material and a brochure from which the participants intend to send a message to the community.

BREAK YOUR BOUNDRIES!

The idea of this project is to build a new way of thinking about youth employability as a tool to develop the participants, and a way of developing the NGO itself.

First, we will look on the right skills (and what is our ROLE on this process - as a youth and NGO) Then we will discuss what are this COMPETENCES that we have and how can we measure them.

After this, youth will experiment it themselves to better understand with others, including INTERCULTURAL COMMUNICATION.

We will also discuss on how young people can express their competences effectively, and how can we support them in understanding the VALUE and gather the evidences of their ability.

This process will be done for 42 youth (18-28 age old) and 7 youth leaders from 7 countries involved in this project, during 11 days of effective activities in August-September 2017 in Romania.

In our opinion, the road to employability or how to get the right job consist in assessing the right skills (required from employers), knowing yourself and the ability to communicate with others.

Youth Today

Our Association has identified among the young people who graduate high school or university a keen sense of disorientation regarding their future.

One of the issues often recognized by young people is that there is a mismatch between the things learnt in school and the things they are required as experience on the labor market.

The information presented during the courses are not relevant to the employers and their requirements are difficult to accomplish for a young graduate.

×

Youth Today

The awareness that the information received in school will not bring an acceptable value for the society leads to an increasing number of graduates who drop courses trying "something else".

However, some of these young people no longer find their place either in school or in society.

This thing has as an effect, besides a low self-esteem, an increase of the unemployment among the youth and a very low level of their participation in the activities of the NGOs and the community.

Together with the youth we have identified a very low degree of understanding the entrepreneurial opportunities offered by volunteering through non-formal education in their personal development.

The lack of support provided to the young people in this direction leads to a static society, aged in terms of the involvement in the society.

The young people will adapt to a new development process where they will be challenged so that their talent and skills to be put into value and to undertake something, to be entrepreneurs, thus contributing to the decrease of unemployment among young people.

We can’t overlook the key concept of entrepreneurship, entrepreneurship education, a concept that supports the young people in order to do something, to put into practice the information, desires and experiences gained for managing their own project with various innovative activities.

By this project a follow-up will be provided contributing to a good collaboration between the direct partners involved in this exchange, who wish to ensure the continuity of the project began with the funding of the Erasmus + project, bringing an adding value to the European community and multiplying the results at as many young people as possible.
